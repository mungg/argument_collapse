You read a public-forum lead essay and classify the underlying debate question it raises by how quickly the answer to that question would change with time, using the FreshQA taxonomy with three labels.

The input is a long lead essay written by a single author. It typically opens with framing, an example, or a current event, then poses or implies a contested public question that response essays will engage with. Your first task is to identify the underlying disputed question the essay raises; your second task is to classify that question's answer by change rate. Anchor on the underlying question — do not classify by how time-bound the essay's framing feels.

THE TEST
Once you have identified the underlying question, imagine ONE thoughtful person who writes a response to that question today. They are asked the SAME question again later. Would they give a substantively different answer?

- "fast_changing":  yes, within a year or less. The answer hinges on a specific unresolved situation — an active negotiation, pending decision, ongoing crisis, or near-term event — whose outcome is likely to settle or materially shift the answer within the year. (Includes: the question becomes moot or shifts to historical retrospective.)
- "slow_changing":  yes, but only over multiple years. The answer would shift gradually as conditions, norms, evidence, or institutions accumulate change.
- "never_changing": no — the answer would essentially be the same regardless of when it is asked. The question is doctrinal, philosophical, mathematical, or about a timeless empirical or moral claim.

DO NOT label based on:
- whether the essay mentions a recent event, date, or named actor.
- whether the essay's opening anecdote or examples reference current circumstances.
- whether the topic happens to be "in the news."

Lead essays almost always anchor in a specific moment, example, or named actor. That anchoring is the essay's opening device, not the underlying question. If the underlying question is structural (about policy design, values, institutions, or long-term strategy) and only the framing references a recent event, classify by the structural question.

Return strict JSON only with this exact schema:
{ "underlying_question": "one-sentence statement of the disputed question the essay raises", "change_rate": "fast_changing" | "slow_changing" | "never_changing", "rationale": "1-2 sentence explanation that names specifically what about the answer would change, and on what timescale" }
