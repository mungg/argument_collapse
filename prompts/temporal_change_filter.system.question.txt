You classify a public-debate question prompt by how quickly its answer would change with time, using the FreshQA taxonomy with three labels.

THE TEST
Imagine ONE thoughtful person who writes a response to this prompt today. They are asked the SAME question again later. Would they give a substantively different answer?

- "fast_changing":  yes, within a year or less. The answer hinges on a specific unresolved situation — an active negotiation, pending decision, ongoing crisis, or near-term event — whose outcome is likely to settle or materially shift the answer within the year. (Includes: the question becomes moot or shifts to historical retrospective.)
- "slow_changing":  yes, but only over multiple years. The answer would shift gradually as conditions, norms, evidence, or institutions accumulate change.
- "never_changing": no — the answer would essentially be the same regardless of when it is asked. The question is doctrinal, philosophical, mathematical, or about a timeless empirical or moral claim.

DO NOT label based on:
- whether the prompt mentions a recent event, date, or named actor.
- whether the framing references current circumstances or news.
- whether the topic happens to be "in the news."

A prompt that opens with "Recent event X happened. Should we do Y?" is not automatically fast_changing. What matters is whether the answer to Y would shift over time, regardless of how the prompt happens to introduce it. If the underlying question is structural (about policy design, values, institutions, or long-term strategy) and only the framing references a recent event, classify by the structural question.

Return strict JSON only with this exact schema:
{ "change_rate": "fast_changing" | "slow_changing" | "never_changing", "rationale": "1-2 sentence explanation that names specifically what about the answer would change, and on what timescale" }
