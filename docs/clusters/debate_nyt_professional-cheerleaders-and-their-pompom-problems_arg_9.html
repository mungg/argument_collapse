Diversified Gemini

"Instead of fighting for better conditions in exploitative professional sports leagues, competitive cheerleaders should pursue careers in the lucrative and respectful private cheerleading industry."

The recent lawsuits filed by professional cheerleaders against their NFL teams have exposed a shocking reality about the glamorous world of the professional sidelines. But for young women currently competing in the highly athletic, newly sanctioned sport of high school and college cheerleading, the takeaway should not be to fight the NFL. The takeaway should be to walk away from it entirely.

The simple economic reality is that the supply of young women who want to be an NFL or NBA cheerleader vastly outstrips the demand. Because of the perceived prestige and glamour associated with the shield and the pom-poms, teams have a bottomless pool of applicants willing to work for pennies. Franchises exploit this dynamic, knowing that if one woman refuses to work for less than minimum wage, a hundred others are waiting to take her place.

True competitive cheerleaders should realize that professional sideline dancing is a dead-end career for a serious athlete. If you want to keep your competitive edge and earn a living in the working world, the rapidly expanding All-Star cheerleading industry is where the actual money and respect lie.

Private cheerleading gyms, coaching, choreography, and event production are booming businesses. Elite college cheerleaders are leveraging their skills to open their own training facilities, run profitable summer camps, or work as commentators for networks like ESPN. They are becoming entrepreneurs in a multi-million-dollar youth sports ecosystem.

Why subject yourself to a degrading team handbook that dictates the color of your nail polish and pays you $50 a game when you could be making a six-figure salary directing a competitive cheer program? The best way for female athletes to preserve their dignity and capitalize on their hard-earned skills is to boycott the exploitative professional sports leagues and build their own empires within the sport they actually love.

Sub-arguments
  1. 01The massive oversupply of applicants for professional cheerleading positions allows NFL and NBA teams to exploit women and pay them less than minimum wage.
  2. 02Professional sideline dancing is a career dead-end that lacks the athletic rigor and professional respect found in competitive cheerleading.
  3. 03The booming All-Star cheerleading industry offers diverse and profitable opportunities in coaching, choreography, and business ownership.
  4. 04Cheerleaders can better preserve their dignity and financial independence by becoming entrepreneurs within the youth sports ecosystem rather than adhering to degrading professional league standards.
