Diversified Gemini

"The United States should remove Christmas from the roster of federal holidays and transition to a strictly civic calendar to uphold the separation of church and state."

The fact that Christmas remains a recognized federal holiday in 2013 is a glaring violation of the spirit, if not the letter, of the First Amendment. When the federal government shuts its doors, suspends mail delivery, and pays its workforce to stay home on December 25th, it is unmistakably elevating one religious tradition above all others. If we are to take the separation of church and state seriously, it is time for the United States to remove Christmas from the roster of federal holidays and commit to a strictly civic calendar.

Defenders of the status quo often argue that Christmas has become a secular holiday, defined more by consumerism and Santa Claus than by the birth of Jesus. But this argument rings hollow to millions of Jewish, Muslim, Hindu, Buddhist, and non-religious Americans. To them, the government’s endorsement of Christmas is a powerful annual reminder of their secondary status in the cultural hierarchy. The question posed—why Christmas but not Mawlid, Yom Kippur, or Diwali?—has no satisfactory answer other than historical Protestant hegemony.

A truly secular, modern democracy should not be in the business of granting paid leave for religious observances. Instead, federal holidays should be exclusively reserved for honoring our shared civic heritage and national history. Days like Independence Day, Thanksgiving, Memorial Day, Veterans Day, Labor Day, and Martin Luther King Jr. Day bind us together as a nation. They celebrate the sacrifices, struggles, and triumphs of the American experiment. They belong to everyone, regardless of faith or creed.

By stripping religious holidays from the federal calendar, we would not be declaring war on religion. On the contrary, we would be privatizing it, which is exactly where faith belongs in a free society. Federal and state workers who wish to observe Christmas, Good Friday, or any other religious day should be encouraged to use their accrued paid time off to do so, just as a Muslim employee currently uses their vacation time to observe Eid al-Fitr.

Reforming our holiday calendar to be strictly secular would align our official government practices with our highest constitutional ideals. It would ensure that the government treats all citizens equally, recognizing that what unites us is our shared civic identity, not our diverse theological beliefs.

Sub-arguments
  1. 01The federal recognition of Christmas violates the First Amendment by elevating one religious tradition above all others.
  2. 02The government's endorsement of a Christian holiday reinforces a cultural hierarchy that treats non-Christian and non-religious citizens as secondary.
  3. 03Federal holidays should be exclusively reserved for honoring shared civic heritage and national history rather than religious observances.
  4. 04Privatizing religious holidays by requiring employees to use personal leave for observance ensures the government treats all faiths and creeds with equal neutrality.
  5. 05A strictly secular holiday calendar aligns government practices with constitutional ideals of equality and shared civic identity.
