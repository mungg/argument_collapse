"The Olympics should eliminate sports that require expensive equipment, private facilities, or specific geographic advantages to ensure the Games are more accessible and meritocratic."

If we are going to eliminate something from the Olympics, let's start with the sports that depend on expensive equipment, private facilities, or geographic accident — the ones that are essentially competitions among rich people from rich countries.

Look at the medal table for equestrian events: Germany, Britain, the Netherlands, Sweden, the United States. Sailing: the same plus Australia and New Zealand. Modern pentathlon, with its bizarre Cold War origins and its requirement that athletes ride, fence, shoot, swim, and run, is a country club triathlon that almost no nation on earth bothers to develop. These sports persist on the Olympic program largely because European aristocrats and their institutional descendants have always sat on the IOC.

Meanwhile, sports that billions of people actually play — cricket, squash, rugby (returning in sevens form, finally), karate — struggle for inclusion. Wrestling, one of the original Olympic events accessible to anyone with a mat and an opponent, gets fewer weight classes every cycle. Baseball and softball were cut in 2005 in part because the IOC felt the major leagues didn't take it seriously enough, while dressage sails on undisturbed.

The Olympics is supposed to be a global festival. It should privilege sports that are globally played, cheap to enter, and meritocratic in the sense that talent from a Kenyan village or a Brazilian favela has some hope of reaching the podium. A horse capable of Olympic dressage costs more than most people in the world earn in a lifetime. That alone should disqualify the sport from a competition that markets itself as a celebration of universal human striving.

Cut equestrian. Cut modern pentathlon. Cut sailing down to one or two classes. Use the spots for sports the world actually plays. The Games would be leaner, fairer, and a lot more honest about what they claim to be.

Sub-arguments
  1. 01Equestrian, sailing, and modern pentathlon are dominated by wealthy nations and persist primarily due to the influence of European aristocrats within the IOC.
  2. 02The current Olympic program excludes or marginalizes globally popular sports like cricket, squash, and wrestling in favor of niche, high-cost activities.
  3. 03The high cost of entry for sports like dressage contradicts the Olympic mission of celebrating universal human striving and global participation.
  4. 04Replacing elite, equipment-heavy sports with widely played activities would make the Games fairer and more representative of the world's population.
