Sub-arguments
  1. 01While American companies create immense value in the Chinese market, China's protectionist policies and lack of intellectual property rights prevent Americans from capturing a fair share of that revenue.
  2. 02By ceding market power and profits to Chinese suppliers, American companies have allowed China to build its own infrastructure and content-creation capabilities, reducing their long-term leverage.
  3. 03The U.S. entertainment industry is increasingly dependent on China for growth as the domestic market matures, making the current imbalance of market access more dangerous.
  4. 04There is a significant lack of reciprocity in investment rules, as China prohibits U.S. ownership of distribution companies while the U.S. allows Chinese investors to acquire American infrastructure.
