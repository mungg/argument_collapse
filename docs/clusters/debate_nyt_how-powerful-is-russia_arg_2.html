Sub-arguments
  1. 01Putin is likely to honor existing international commitments and avoid unnecessary conflict with Washington to focus on Russia's socio-economic performance.
  2. 02There is potential for bilateral cooperation on regional security issues like Syria and Iran, provided the United States avoids unilateral military intervention.
  3. 03Economic engagement could deepen if Putin opens Russia's energy sector to American investment, moving the relationship beyond traditional military concerns.
  4. 04Domestic questions regarding the legitimacy of Putin's election could pressure the U.S. administration to distance itself, potentially triggering Putin's fears of Western-led regime change.

Sub-arguments
  1. 01Putin's hostile campaign rhetoric was a calculated strategy to create an external enemy and rally Russians around the flag during a period of internal political instability.

  4. 04Despite public disagreements, Russia shares strategic interests with the U.S. regarding nuclear nonproliferation and maintaining stability in the Middle East.
