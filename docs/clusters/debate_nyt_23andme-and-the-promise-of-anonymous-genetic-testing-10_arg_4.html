Diversified Gemini

"Commercial companies should not be allowed to share genetic information for research because DNA is inherently identifiable and its commodification poses a severe threat to individual and familial privacy."

The premise that consumer genetic data can be safely shared for research because it is "anonymous" relies on a fundamental misunderstanding of what DNA is. Your genome is the ultimate personally identifiable information. It cannot be permanently anonymized, and allowing commercial companies to monetize this data under the guise of scientific progress is a massive threat to civil liberties.

In recent years, researchers have repeatedly demonstrated that "de-identified" genomic data is a myth. By cross-referencing supposedly anonymous DNA databases with publicly available genealogical registries and basic demographic data like age and zip code, scientists have successfully re-identified individuals and their extended families. When a consumer spits in a tube and agrees to 23andMe’s terms of service, they are not just compromising their own privacy; they are making a permanent, irrevocable decision that exposes the biological secrets of their parents, siblings, and future children.

Furthermore, we must examine the business model driving this data collection. 23andMe is not a philanthropic research institute; it is a for-profit corporation. The $99 consumers pay for a spit kit is heavily subsidized by the company’s true revenue stream: selling access to its massive genomic database to massive pharmaceutical companies. Consumers are paying a corporation for the privilege of becoming its unpaid, uncompensated product.

Once this data is sold and shared, the consumer loses all control over how it is used. Will it be used to cure a rare disease, or will it be used by life insurance companies to adjust actuarial tables in the future? Our current legal framework, including laws like the Genetic Information Nondiscrimination Act (GINA), is woefully inadequate for the era of big genetic data, as it does not cover life, disability, or long-term care insurance. Until we have ironclad, comprehensive federal protections regarding the commodification and weaponization of genetic data, commercial sharing must be halted.
