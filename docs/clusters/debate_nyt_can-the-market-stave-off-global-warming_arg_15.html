Diversified Gemini

"The Obama administration’s cap-and-trade rule should be rejected in favor of free-market solutions because it is an executive overreach that will damage the economy without significantly impacting the global climate."

The Obama administration’s new EPA rule is a staggering display of executive overreach that will inflict profound damage on the American economy while doing virtually nothing to alter the global climate. By attempting to backdoor a national cap-and-trade system after Congress explicitly rejected such a scheme in 2010, the administration is declaring a regulatory war on affordable energy.

The fundamental flaw in this approach is that it treats cheap energy as a vice rather than the lifeblood of economic prosperity. Cap-and-trade, by definition, works by artificially restricting the supply of energy, thereby driving up prices. When utility companies are forced to purchase permits to generate electricity, they do not absorb those costs; they pass them directly to consumers. This acts as a highly regressive tax, punishing the poorest Americans who spend the highest percentage of their income on heating and cooling their homes, and driving energy-intensive manufacturing jobs overseas to countries with no environmental standards.

The irony of this heavy-handed government intervention is that the free market is already solving the problem. Over the last decade, U.S. carbon dioxide emissions have plummeted to their lowest levels in 20 years. This was not achieved by UN treaties, EPA mandates, or complex trading schemes. It was achieved by American wildcatters and innovators who cracked the code of hydraulic fracturing and horizontal drilling. The shale revolution has produced an ocean of cheap, clean-burning natural gas, which has rapidly displaced coal in the electricity sector through pure market economics.

We are greening our grid not by making coal artificially expensive, but by making natural gas abundant and cheap. If we allow the market to function, this transition will continue, and advances in technology will naturally integrate competitive renewables when they make economic sense.

Imposing a top-down, bureaucratic cap-and-trade system via executive fiat will only stifle economic growth, kill jobs, and spike electricity rates. We cannot regulate our way to global cooling. We should embrace the free market, not shackle it with punitive carbon caps.

Sub-arguments
  1. 01Cap-and-trade acts as a regressive tax that disproportionately harms low-income Americans and drives manufacturing jobs to countries with lower environmental standards.
  2. 02The free market is already successfully reducing carbon emissions through the shale revolution and the resulting abundance of cheap, clean-burning natural gas.
  3. 03Top-down bureaucratic regulations stifle the economic growth and innovation necessary to naturally integrate renewable energy sources.
  4. 04Artificially restricting energy supplies treats affordable energy as a vice rather than the essential driver of economic prosperity.
