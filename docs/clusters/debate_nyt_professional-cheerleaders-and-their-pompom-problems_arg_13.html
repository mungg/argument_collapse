Diversified Gemini

"To achieve professional respect and fair pay, professional sports leagues must replace the current model of sideline dancers with elite, co-ed acrobatic stunt teams modeled after competitive college programs."

To understand why professional cheerleaders are treated so poorly, we have to acknowledge a fundamental truth: the NFL and NBA do not actually employ "cheerleaders" in the athletic sense of the word. They employ dancers and models.

When New York recently recognized high school cheerleading as a competitive sport, they were acknowledging the intense gymnastics, tumbling, and high-flying stunting that define modern competitive cheer. You see this on ESPN during the college championships. It is a dangerous, thrilling, and highly athletic endeavor.

But when you look at an NFL sideline, you do not see elite stunting. You see choreographed dance routines performed by women selected primarily for their physical appearance and ability to fit into a two-piece uniform. The professional leagues have stripped the actual "sport" out of cheerleading, leaving behind a Vegas-style revue. Because the franchises treat the role as mere sideline eye candy, they feel justified in paying the performers in "exposure" and "glamour" rather than a professional athletic salary.

If cheerleaders want to keep their competitive edge in the working world, the job itself must be redefined. Professional sports teams should abandon the outdated 1970s model of the sideline dancer and replace them with co-ed, acrobatic stunt teams modeled after the elite college programs.

If an NFL franchise hired a genuine competitive cheerleading squad—featuring male and female athletes executing basket tosses, pyramids, and elite tumbling passes—the conversation would instantly change. The athletes would be undeniable assets to the game-day entertainment, recognized for their specialized physical skills rather than their measurements. Until the professional leagues are forced to value the athletic substance of cheerleading over its aesthetic appeal, the women on the sidelines will never be granted the respect, or the paychecks, of true athletes.

Sub-arguments
  1. 01Current professional cheerleading focuses on physical appearance and choreographed dance rather than the high-level gymnastics and stunting found in competitive sports.
  2. 02Professional franchises justify low wages and poor treatment by viewing cheerleaders as aesthetic 'eye candy' rather than specialized athletes.
  3. 03Adopting a co-ed, stunt-heavy model would force leagues to value cheerleaders for their undeniable athletic skills and specialized physical contributions to game-day entertainment.
  4. 04The transition from a 'Vegas-style revue' to a genuine athletic endeavor is necessary to secure the same recognition and compensation granted to other professional athletes.
