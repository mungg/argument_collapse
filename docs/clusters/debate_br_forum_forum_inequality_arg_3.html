Sub-arguments
  1. 01Claims of rising income inequality are overblown and misleading because they rely on household data rather than individual income, which has actually become more equal.
  2. 02The growth of wealth at the top does not come at the expense of the poor, as all income groups have seen their material standing improve over recent decades.
  3. 03The gap in personal well-being between the rich and the average American has narrowed significantly due to technological progress and access to essential goods and services.
  4. 04The United States remains a highly mobile society where individuals frequently move out of the bottom income quintiles, suggesting there is no permanent underclass.
  5. 05Market distortions like the higher-education cartel should be dismantled to increase competition and opportunity rather than to achieve the secondary goal of equalizing incomes.
  6. 06Focusing on CEO salaries and redistributive taxation is a distraction from the more urgent need to reform K-12 education and remove barriers to entrepreneurship.
