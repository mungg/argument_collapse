"The presidential debate system requires structural reform through the replacement of the Commission on Presidential Debates and the implementation of more inclusive participant selection and substantive formatting rules."

The presidential debate system needs structural reform that addresses both the selection of participants and the format of questioning. The Commission on Presidential Debates should be replaced with a more accountable institution that includes broader stakeholder representation, including voting rights advocates, election administration experts, and civic education professionals, not just representatives of the two major parties.

More specifically, the polling thresholds used to determine which candidates participate in nationally televised debates should be modified to include alternative indicators of public interest, such as grassroots fundraising totals and endorsements from a broader range of elected officials. The current threshold system effectively privileges name recognition over policy substance or organizational strength.

The debate format itself should include a mandatory fact-checking component. When candidates make false factual claims, a nonpartisan panel should provide immediate correction, and these corrections should receive equal prominence with the original false claims. This would reduce the incentive to make claims that will later be quietly corrected after having served their political purpose.

Finally, debates should be shorter—ninety minutes rather than two hours. Shorter formats require more careful time allocation, reducing the ability of moderators to control the narrative through selective questioning. Candidates would have more time per response, creating space for substantive discussion within a format that maintains audience engagement.

These structural changes would not eliminate conflict or spontaneity from debates. They would simply shift the framework within which conflict occurs, creating more opportunities for policy substance to emerge as candidates compete for voter attention.
