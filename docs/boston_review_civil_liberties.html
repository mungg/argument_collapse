Civil Liberties after 9/11

Boston Review · December 2002 · Lead essay by David Cole · 9 respondents
Lead essay (David Cole)

The Bush Administration's post-9/11 measures have largely targeted noncitizens — through detentions, deportations, secret hearings, and selective enforcement. Cole argues that these "small compromises" with liberty erode protections for everyone and rely on a false distinction between citizens and others.
