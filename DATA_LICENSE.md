# Data licensing

This release combines materials with different rights. We list them separately below.

1. **Original NYT *Room for Debate* debate prompts.** Included as `question_text` in `debates.jsonl.gz`.
2. **Original Boston Review forum lead essays.** Included as `lead_essay_text` in `debates.jsonl.gz`.
3. **Original human responder essays** (both venues). **Metadata only.** No body text. Reconstruct using `scripts/refetch_human_essays.py`.
4. **Derived data and code.** This includes LLM essays, position guides, argument extractions, pairwise judgments, and structure annotations produced by the authors.

## NYT *Room for Debate* debate prompts (`debates.jsonl.gz#question_text`)

> © The New York Times Company. Used as the subject of academic commentary and analysis. Not redistributed under any open license.

NYT's [Terms of Service](https://help.nytimes.com/hc/en-us/articles/115014893428-Terms-of-Service), sections 2.1 to 2.3, reserve copyright and prohibit redistribution of NYT Content without prior written permission from the NYT Rights and Permissions Department.

The short debate prompts included in `debates.jsonl.gz` are reproduced for the purpose of identifying each debate and supporting academic analysis under the fair-use doctrine (17 U.S.C. section 107). The four-factor analysis favors use here because the purpose is non-commercial research, the prompts are short, *Room for Debate* was retired in 2018 and is no longer actively monetized, and the use does not substitute for the original.

If you are an NYT rights holder requesting removal of a specific prompt, please open an issue on the repository. We will remove it within 7 days.

NYT human responder essays are not redistributed. Only their metadata is included. See "Human responder essays" below.

Author attribution is preserved in the `lead_essay_authors` field of `debates.jsonl.gz`. When citing or reusing a lead essay, please attribute the named author(s) and Boston Review as the original venue.

## Human responder essays (both venues)

> Body text is not redistributed in this release. Only metadata (slug, author, date, word count, bio where available) is included in `human_essays.jsonl.gz`.

To rebuild the responder corpus locally, run `scripts/refetch_human_essays.py`. Researchers are responsible for following each publisher's Terms of Service.

**URL availability.** The original scraper did not preserve per-essay URLs, so `source_url` is currently `null`. The re-fetcher tries to rebuild URLs from each essay's date and slug. If it cannot, it logs the essay for manual recovery. We will publish a verified URL list once available.
